Why Exercise?

According to Mayo Clinic, regular exercise offers a multitude of benefits for both physical and mental well-being. It aids in weight management by burning calories and can prevent or manage various health conditions including heart disease, high blood pressure, and diabetes. Exercise boosts mood, energy levels, and promotes better sleep, while also enhancing sexual function and providing opportunities for social interaction. Following recommended guidelines for aerobic and strength training activities not only improves overall health but also adds enjoyment to daily life, making exercise an essential component of a happier and healthier lifestyle.

Chest Exercises

Personally, chest is one of my favorite muscle groups to train because I've always excelled at it ever since I started lifting. And I feel there is low potential for injury if your form is good. There are a handful of different movements/exercises that you can do to target different parts of your chest. Flat barbell bench press is the first thing people think of when you talk about bench press. There's incline and decline presses and you can even add in dumbbell work to allow more movement between each arm. Feel free to check out each link for more information about each exercise.

Shoulder Exercises

Shoulder play a huge role in chest movements/exercises. During flat bench press you are actually using a lot of your front deltoid (shoulder muscle) to assist your chest to move that weight up. My favorite shoulder exercise is called the lateral raise. You take some pretty lightweight dumbbells, have your arms fall to your side and raise your arms up as if you were transitioning into a T pose. What's important about this exercise is that you don't swing your arms too much and that you lift with your shoulder and not your wrists. Consistent and intentional shoulder workouts will lead to a big increase in shoulder size and strength.

Tricep Exercises

Triceps are another muscle group that assists heavily in the bench press. If you haven't noticed but the reason these muscle groups are under the "Push" category is because these movements involve pushing weight away from you. These muscle groups are connectec with each other in a way. Bench press is a good movement that can help grow/strengthen your triceps but there are some other movements that help a lot more. Tricep pushdown is probably the most common tricep exercise that you'll see a lot of people do. You'll typically need a cable connected to some weight that's about eye level to you. Connected to that cable is a set of ropes that you grab with each hand. You tuck your elbows into your side and try to straighten your arms without moving your elbows, which then focuses primarily on your triceps.

Back Exercises

Most back movements, if not all, will involve you pulling the weight closer to you to ensure contracted back muscles. Think of a pull up for example, you pull yourself up to squeeze and contract your back muscles to give them that desired burn. Pull ups are very common back exercises and so is the lat pulldown. Think of it as a seated pull up but you're bringing the weight down to you. It allows you to adjust the weight to your desired limit and gives you the chance to go at your pace to effectively control your back muscles.

Bicep Exercises

Biceps aren't the funnest muscle group to train, they're on the more boring side in my opinion. But working them out is just as important as every other muscle group. If you were to train every other muscle in your arm other than your bicep then you'd start have muscle imbalance in your arms, which is something you for sure want to avoid. A very good bicep exercise is your common dumbbell bicep curl. This will allow you to focus hard on your eccentric and concentric movements with each arm (the rise and fall movements against the weight).

Quad Exercises

The quads make up a large percentage of all the muscles in your legs and is a muscle group that takes discipline and hard work to see the results. They are located on the front part of your thigh and you can feel them flexed when you straighten your leg out all the way. There are a lot of different movements and ways you can target your quads but the most effective way seems to be the squat. The squat works more than just your quads but if you want a good burn on them then definitely stick with the squat.

Hamstring Exercises

The hamstrings consist of the back part of thighs and play a big role in bending down/over to pick stuff up. That super good stretch that you have after a really long roadtrip comes from being able to straighten your legs and relieve some tension in your hamstrings. My favorite hamstring exercise is called the romanian deadlift. It's very similar to a regular deadlift but with less back movement. Your legs barely bend which puts all the weight on the back side of your legs, or otherwise known as your hamstrings.

Calf Exercises

The calves lie on the back side of your lower leg. When you stand up on your tip toes you are actually flexing your calf muscles to allow your feet to do that. Working out your calf muscles are pretty straight forward, there's really only a handful of movements that target your calf muscles, and all of them include going up on your tip toes. They are a hard muscle group to train and grow.
